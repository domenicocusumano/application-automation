Pipeline
Score threshold
Minimum score (0–10) Claude must give a job in the resume-building phase for it to be accepted. Lower = more jobs processed; higher = more selective.
Title Filters
Role keywords (must match)
A job title must contain at least one of these phrases to be considered a candidate. Change this to match your target role — e.g. replace "product manager" lines with "software engineer" or "data scientist". One phrase per line. Leave empty to accept all titles.
Excluded titles (exact phrase)
Titles containing any of these phrases are rejected outright, even if they match a role keyword above. Useful for filtering adjacent roles (e.g. "program manager" when searching for PMs). One phrase per line.
Excluded title words (whole word)
Titles containing any of these as a whole word are rejected. Useful for blocking adjacent professions. For example, "engineer" blocks "AI Engineer" but not "Engineering Manager". Comma-separated.
Scoring & Filters
Seniority tiers
Keywords that boost a job's score. Listed top-to-bottom: first entry = highest score bonus. Jobs without any keyword are not excluded — they just score lower (base 3.0/10). One keyword or phrase per line.
Location preferences
Comma-separated location keywords (case-insensitive). Jobs whose location doesn't match any keyword are filtered out before scoring. "United States" with no major city name is always kept as likely-remote.
Salary minimum ($)
Minimum acceptable annual salary. Only applies when a listing includes structured salary data. Set to 0 to disable. (Most LinkedIn and Built-in listings don't include salary — this is for future use.)
LinkedIn
Runs the LinkedIn Recommended feed (Phase 1) and keyword search (Phase 2). Uncheck to skip all LinkedIn scraping in the pipeline.
Also scrapes the LinkedIn Top Applicant collection (Phase 0) before the Recommended feed. Requires LinkedIn Premium.
Search term
Keywords used for the Phase 2 LinkedIn job search. Change this to broaden or narrow the types of roles LinkedIn returns.
Built-in
When enabled, Run Pipeline scrapes Built-in.com instead of LinkedIn — both cannot run simultaneously. The LinkedIn pipeline is skipped entirely.
Search URL
Paste your Built-in.com job search results page URL here. The scraper will walk through all paginated result pages.
Google Sheets
Spreadsheet URL
Paste the full URL of your Google Sheet (e.g. https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/SHEET_ID/edit). The sheet tracks applied and skipped jobs to prevent duplicates across all scrapers.

How to grant access: Open your sheet → Share → paste the client_email value from your google_credentials.json file → set role to Viewer (read-only dedup) or Editor (to allow writing results). The service account email looks like name@project.iam.gserviceaccount.com.
